Patna, the erstwhile City of Pataliputra lies to the south of the Ganges River. This ancient city has seen the rise & fall of many empires, and has served as the centre of culture, spirituality, politics and commerce for many years. Since Independence, Patna has been the state capital of Bihar.On the outskirts of Patna and explorable as day tours are the Buddhist, Hindu and Jain pilgrimage centres of Vaishali, Rajgir, Nalanda, Bodh Gaya and Pawapuri. Patna City is also a sacred city for the Sikhs, it is here that the tenth Sikh Guru, Guru Gobind Singh was born.

Patna is a land of delectable cuisines most of which are predominantly vegetarian. Litti Chokhais the introduction to the staple diet of the town. It is a savoury wheat and sattu ball dipped in ghee. The ball full of spices and a mix of several flavors, is served with cooked and mashed vegetables.The regular food consist of seasonal vegetables and pulses, Everyday food regulates around roti, daal, bhaat and sabzi.In addition to this, local people are extremely fond of mutton, fish and fried chicken dishes. The flavours are inspired from Bengali and North Indian cuisines and are a lip-smacking fusion.In addition to this, Patna has a special fondness for sweet dishes. Naivedyam is a popular dish made from besan and dry fruits. Other famous sweets are Chandrakala, Gujjia, Khaja, Kesar Peda, Dal Peda, Malpua, Parwal ki mithai etc.
